SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY

We work with suppliers who, like us, operate with the future in mind

Our commitment to sustainability means we require our suppliers to maintain fair labor practices, foster worker safety, protect the environment and actively assess and manage risk.

To ensure suppliers take these measures, we evaluate them through our Supplier Social Responsibility Program, which is part of the AbbVie Supplier Sustainability program.

If our suppliers need support to achieve compliance or have an interest in establishing higher standards, our Environmental, Health and Safety (EHS) team also offers pro bono services, including EHS audits and sustainability training.

SUPPLIER RISK MANAGEMENT

We assess and audit our critical suppliers

To ensure continuous supply to our customers and patients, we monitor the financial health of our suppliers, conduct quality compliance audits of third parties, and participate in various supply chain security programs around the globe, e.g., the Customs Trade Partnership Against Terrorism (CTPAT) in the U.S., Partners in Protection (PiP) in Canada, and several Authorized Economic Operator (AEO) programs.

AbbVie continuously evaluates available programs as part of our commitment to ensure the security of the supply chain and the ability to deliver medicines and products to patients. Participation in these programs requires AbbVie to work jointly with critical suppliers to perform risk assessments, and where required, we support those suppliers in developing business continuity plans to ensure security processes and procedures that protect the integrity of the supply chain are in place.

Our technology platform allows us to evaluate risk across a number of critical domains, develop and implement risk mitigation plans, monitor our suppliers’ performance, and manage potential disruptions to our supply chain. This platform, in coordination with subject matter experts in various partner organizations within AbbVie, provides us with a broad line of sight into risks across the global supply chain on a real-time basis, offering 24/7 alerts to key decision-makers in vulnerable locations.

What is the Drug Supply Chain Security Act?

The Drug Supply Chain Security Act is a federal law enacted in 2013 as part of the Drug Quality and Security Act (DQSA). The DSCSA establishes requirements for tracing prescription drug products through the pharmaceutical supply chain in the United States, with the main goal of enhancing the security of the drug supply and helping to prevent counterfeit, stolen, contaminated, or otherwise harmful drugs from reaching patients. It sets standards for product identification, tracing, verification, and licensing for manufacturers, repackagers, wholesale distributors, and dispensers.

Legal reference: DSCSA is Title II of the Drug Quality and Security Act (DQSA), which is Public Law 113-54, enacted in 2013. The DSCSA amended the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (FD&C Act), and its provisions are primarily found in sections 581 and 582 of the FD&C Act (21 U.S.C. § 360eee and 21 U.S.C. § 360eee-1).

As an Authorized Trading Partner, how do I get on-boarded to receive Item-level DSCSA Transaction Information for my in-scope AbbVie purchases?

AbbVie provides serialized transaction information either through Electronic Product Code Information Services (EPCIS) or via web portal PDF download.

Please email AbbVie Drug Traceability at DrugTraceability@AbbVie.com. Include the following information in your email:

  • Corporate Name, Address, and Global Location Number (GLN)
  • Location master data for all Ship-To locations, including name, street address, GLN, and one of the following identifiers for each location: AbbVie account number, DEA Number, State License Number, or HIN.
  • Indicate whether you want EPCIS or Portal access.
  • For EPCIS, identify your Third-Party Service Provider or internal technical contact name and email.

Who do I contact with EDI Questions?

Please email AbbVie EDI at AbbVie_EDI@AbbVie.com.

How do I report issues/exceptions with DSCSA Transaction Information?

Please email AbbVie Drug Traceability at DrugTraceability@AbbVie.com and provide Purchase Order (PO) and Delivery Number, along with details on the issue encountered. Providing photos of the product in question will generally speed resolution.

How does AbbVie support Enhanced Verification?

An Enhanced Verification request may be initiated by an Authorized Trading Partner (ATP) that has the product in their physical possession or by a Regulator. AbbVie uses the VRS (Verification Router Service) for automatic responses and can be used by ATPs that are in the network. The VRS provides a “True” or “False” response if the four (4) Product Identifier attributes (Lot, Expiry, GTIN, Serial Number) in the 2D DataMatrix barcode and Human Readable text correspond with the information in the AbbVie database and the serial number is designated as “Active”.

How do I submit a Manual Product Verification Request?

If the Authorized Trading Partner or Regulator does not have access to the VRS, please email the Product Identifier information (Lot, Expiry, GTIN, Serial Number) and/or provide a photograph of the 2D DataMatrix barcode and Human Readable Text to AbbVie Drug Traceability at DrugTraceability@AbbVie.com.

How do I submit a Product Trace Request?

A Product Trace is executed to support suspect, illegitimate, or recalled product investigations or for Regulator compliance audits. A Trace is gathering information about products, supply chain partners, and change of ownership. The Trace response will include the Product Information including serial number, and the “sold to” and “ship to” information from AbbVie to the customer.

A manual Product Trace request may be initiated by an Authorized Trading Partner or Regulator for a suspect and / or illegitimate product investigation, recalled product investigation or audit. Please follow these steps to initiate a Product Trace request with AbbVie:

  • Please send an email for a product trace to DrugTraceability@AbbVie.com.

  • Please add to the email Subject line: Product Trace <Your Company or Regulator Name>

  • Provide at minimum the following information:

    • Person or Department Name
    • Contact Phone Number
    • Requester Corporate GLN, if ATP
    • Response Requested: All known owners or last owner with TI information
    • Reasons for investigation: Suspect or Illegitimate Product, Recall, or Compliance Audit
    • 3911 Incident Number, if Applicable
    • Name of Product/ Strength to be traced with the GTIN (or NDC and Unit of Measure), Serial Number, Lot, and Expiry

AbbVie will acknowledge receipt of your trace request. Prior to any response AbbVie will confirm the requester is an Authorized Trading Partner or Regulator. If AbbVie requires further information, you will be contacted, so please ensure you provide a name, phone number and email in your request.
